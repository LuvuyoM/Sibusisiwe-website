Our History

Founding

The school was founded by Sibusisiwe Violet Makhanya [ 1894 – 1971 ] who used her house to render the initial classes for the community of Umbumbulu. She was a social worker and philanthropist. When formal schooling programmes started, the school was then named after its founder and became known as Sibusisiwe Secondary school. In 1954 the school was officially opened as a secondary school. In 1991, the school received funding from Toyota South Africa for the establishment of the Technical division in the school curriculum. This funding facilitated the introduction of a full package of 5 technical subjects. It was decided that the school will offer both academic and technical education. The school then became known as Sibusisiwe Comprehensive Technical High school.



Current Day

Fast forward to 2005, after some years of instability the current school Principal was appointed. It was the start of a new life for the school. Matric results shot up from 34% to 72%. A solid organizational culture began to take shape with strong academic foundations. Over the years the school has become the beacon of hope for the people of Umbumbulu and this gives true meaning to the dream of its founder.

In the last 15 years since 2005 the school has become the centre for academic excellence in the wider Umlazi District with Matric Pass Rate stabilizing between the 90% - 98% in the last 10 years. This school now has 1 785 learners ( 2020 ) and is a Maths Only school. Our curriculum package has a wide range of subjects including Computer Applications Technology ( CAT ), Maritime Economics, Engineering Graphics & Design, 4 Technical Subjects and most of the conventional academic subjects. Despite the very large numbers in our classes, our school has been consistent in academic performance. We remain true to our vision of academic excellence and defeating the odds of being a rural school.
